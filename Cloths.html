The Rabari tribe of India is known for their distinctive and colorful clothing. The traditional attire of Rabari women is a long skirt called ghagra, a blouse called kanchli, and a dupatta, which is a long scarf draped over the head and shoulders. The Rabari men wear a long shirt called kedia, which is paired with a dhoti, a long piece of cloth wrapped around the waist and legs.

One of the most distinctive aspects of Rabari clothing is the embroidery work. The women of the community are skilled in embroidery and use it to adorn their clothing and household items. The embroidery work is typically done in bright and vibrant colors and features intricate designs inspired by nature. The embroidery work is often done on the edges of the ghagra, kanchli, and dupatta, as well as on the sleeves and neckline of the blouse.

The Rabari community also uses mirror work in their embroidery, which adds to the vibrancy of their clothing. The mirrors are typically small and round and are sewn onto the fabric in unique patterns, creating a dazzling effect. The mirror work is often used in combination with embroidery work, adding to the richness and beauty of the clothing.

Overall, the clothing of the Rabari tribe is a beautiful expression of their culture and heritage. The bright colors, intricate embroidery work, and mirror work reflect the community's love of life and celebration, as well as their deep connection to their land and animals.
